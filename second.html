My Co-op Experiences

Lytica Therapeutics

When I first started at Lytica Therapeutics, I had almost no experience working in a laboratory setting, and since it was a start-up, which means that it was a very fast-paced environment, I was able to learn many new technical skills and analytical skill. I will first talk about the new techniques. First, I learned how to culture mammalian cell lines, both adherent (sticking to the bottom of the vessel) and suspension cells (floating in media throughout the vessel). Next, I learned different biological assays to test the efficacy of the their drug candidates, such as Cell-Titer Glo, western blots, and flow cytometry. Cell-Titer Glo measures the proliferation of cells by quantifying cellular ATP production; western blots quantify protein expression; flow cytometry can be used to quantify fluorescent molecule binding (surface) and uptake (internalization) at the single-cell level by measuring Mean Fluorescent Intensity (MFI). Additionally, one of the more interesting pieces of information that I learned is the bystander effect, which is crucial mechanism where the potent, released cytotoxic payload diffuses from targeted cancer cells to kill neighboring antigen-negative or low-expression cells, overcoming tumor heterogeneity, but in a non-specific manner. Therefore, it can increase the efficacy of the drug, but it also has the potential to cause harmful side-effects.

Tango Therapeutics

Since Tango Therapeutics was my second co-op, I had more experience, but I wanted to expand my skillset, particularly learning more about how CRISPR gene editing worked and how it can be used to validate drug therapeutics. The team that I joined was called functional genomics, which primarily conducted reserach using CRISPR editing in vivo to discover potential targets that are implicated in various cancers that can then be target validation and potential drug development. During this time, I also got exposure to lots of other types of techniques, one of which was in vivo work, in which I measured and weighed mice who were used as models for tumor development after injection with specific engineered cell line. I unfortunately did not have time to do surgeries with the mice, but I shadowed a lot of the mouse work from my manager and co-workers. Additionally, I also saw this as a perfect opportunity to level up my bioinformatics skills and see how these digital workflows actually translate into real-world biological discoveries, so I shadowed the work of the bioinformatics team to see how they analyzed the results of my project.
